About Me

I am a project manager with a passion for front-end development. I lead teams of developers to build websites for small and big businesses alike. I like to make things, fix things, and help others - especially through technology.

Work:
I co-founded Nullipotent [nəlipōt(ə)nt], a New York based technology company, in 2013. As Vice President, I work with our clients to encapsulate their brand and the core of their company’s ideology through major website redesigns, mobile app development, and experiential design. I've enjoyed learning to wear different hats while building a startup. I love to roll up my sleeves and delve into challenges to find creative solutions.

Volunteer:
For the past several years I've taught computer classes to seniors through NY Cares. I am currently teaching English language literacy, through that same organization. I also volunteer with Open House New York for OHNY Weekend in the fall. I commit myself to giving back wherever I can, and firmly believe that everyone stands to benefit through volunteering and helping others.

Leisure:
When I'm not working or volunteering I can be found exploring the city I love: museums, restaurants, Broadway shows, and galleries. But I am equally happy hiking in the great outdoors of the Hudson River Valley. I often experiment in the kitchen, my friends and family the willing tasters. And nothing beats a thick history book and a good cup of coffee on a rainy day.
